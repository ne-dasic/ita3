Mihajlo Idvorski Pupin





Mihajlo Idvorski Pupin, Ph.D., LL.D. (Serbian Cyrillic: Mihajlo Idvorski Pupin ; 9 October 1858[3][4] â€“ 12 March 1935), also known as Michael I. Pupin was a Serbian American physicist, physical chemist.Pupin is best known for his numerous patents, including a means of greatly extending the range of long-distance telephone communication by placing loading coils (of wire) at predetermined intervals along the transmitting wire (known as "pupinization"). Pupin was a founding member of National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics (NACA) on 3 March 1915, which later became NASA.
